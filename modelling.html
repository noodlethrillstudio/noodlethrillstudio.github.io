Cyclist

This was a project I started to get to grips with all the different elements of blender, taking it from concept, modelling, rigging, to animation and lighting. I'm particularly happy with the latice deformer on the tires, allowing the tires to spin whilst staying deformed in the same spot.

The Balloon

These models were created as a part of the NextGen bootcamp, and involved sculpting, retopologising, box modelling, and texturing and rigging.

The buildings below were modelled and textured by Ellen Paynter, but assembled by me using Geometry nodes. We decided to use this method to let us create some slightly randomised buildings, and I wanted a good excuse to try out geometry nodes. Overall, they had some great advantages over scripting in ease of use, but did require more complicated node setups for something that could be achieved in fewer lines of code. It worked well in this case, as it allowed the people building the environments to edit things easily.

A challenge here was creating a way for the textures to be changed per building instead of per texture, as the Geo Node editor doesn't work well with textures or colours in some aspects. In the end I took the "long quick" route and just duplicated a number of the textures and attached hue, saturation, and value inputs and manually attached them to iterations of different buildings, giving us over a hundred buildings to pick from without too much time or effort.

The titular Balloon. It was a simple model and rig, just a low poly body and a curve object for the string, but had some nice spline controllers that took a bit of fiddling with but could give some nice results.
